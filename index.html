Phase 2: The Art of the Summary

A good professional summary is all about efficiency. It strips away the "fluff" and leaves only the core facts.

✅ A Summary INCLUDES:

  • Who was involved.
  • Basic facts about what happened.
  • Results or effects of the event.

❌ A Summary EXCLUDES:

  • Minor details (times, specific names if unimportant).
  • Long explanations or direct quotes.
  • The summarizer's personal opinions.

Phase 3: Noun Suffixes

We often need to change verbs or adjectives into nouns when discussing abstract concepts like resilience, success, or fame.

💡 Pro-Tip: Identifying Nouns

If you can put "the", "a", or "my/his/her" in front of the word, you need the noun form, not the adjective!
❌ "She showed a lot of confident." (Confident is an adjective)
✅ "She showed a lot of confidence." (Confidence is the noun)

Phase 4: Reported Speech (The Backshift)

When summarizing what someone else said, we use reported speech. Because the original statement happened in the past, we usually shift the tenses "one step back in time."

🔄 The Backshift Rules

  • Present → Simple Past
    "I am tired" → She said she was tired.
  • Simple Past → Past Perfect
    "I won" → He said he had won.
  • Will → Would / Can → Could
    "I will go" → She said she would go.

🎙️ MAKE IT REAL

Native English speakers do not always backshift in casual conversation, especially if the fact is still true right now.

"She told me that she will call me tomorrow." (Acceptable if tomorrow hasn't happened yet!)

Phase 5: The "Crazy House" Report

It's time to synthesize your skills. Read the article parts about Brian Lee. Then, write a professional 4-5 sentence summary using reported speech.

📖 Read "The Crazy House" Story

Brian Lee, a high school art teacher, painted his beige house in rainbow stripes a year ago. Photos went viral, and while some loved it, many made negative comments. Brian's neighbors sued him to force him to repaint, claiming the house disturbed the neighborhood because of all the tourists taking photos. However, they lost the lawsuit.

Brian admitted that the publicity had been difficult for his family, but he insisted that being in the news had a positive side. He decided to start a house painting business to attract people who wanted to be different. Brian reported that his company had painted over sixty houses in five states in the past six months.

📋 Drafting Checklist

  • Combine the Who, What, and Results. Ignore minor details.
  • Use at least two different reporting verbs (e.g., admitted, claimed, reported, explained).
  • Ensure you apply the correct backshift to the tenses.
📝 Sample Answer:
Brian Lee painted his house in rainbow colors, causing photos to go viral. His neighbors sued him, claiming that the tourists disturbed the neighborhood, but Brian won the lawsuit. He admitted that the negative attention was hard, but explained that it gave him a business idea. He later reported that his new house-painting business had been highly successful.
